Back in 2018, something exciting sizzled in Maharlika Village, Taguig City—the birth of Maharlika Food Street. This delicious venture was the brainchild of Nurizam N. Khusin, a food-loving entrepreneur with a knack for bringing people together.

Go Back

Nurizam had a dream: to create a spot where the fantastic flavors of Filipino cuisine could shine while being mindful of dietary preferences. And so, in the heart of Maharlika Village, she opened a place that welcomed everyone and celebrated diversity.

Go Back

What made Maharlika Food Street stand out? It's a halal restaurant. That means the dishes follow Islamic dietary rules, making it an inclusive spot for folks with various food choices. 

Go Back

Choosing Maharlika Village as the home base was a no-brainer. Nurizam wanted her joint to be part of the local community, and Maharlika Village, known for its tight-knit vibe, was the perfect fit.

Go Back

The journey began with Nurizam crafting a menu that blended classic Filipino recipes with halal goodness. Picture skewers of perfectly marinated meat and bowls of flavorful stews—all designed to make your taste buds dance. 

Go Back

Opening day was a blast. The locals, curious foodies, and families all turned up, ready to dive into the unique combo of halal and Filipino flavors. The restaurant quickly became Taguig City's go-to place, thanks to its warm vibe and the tempting smells wafting from the kitchen.

Go Back

Maharlika Food Street wasn't just a restaurant; it was a friendly spot where everyone could enjoy a good meal together. With her commitment to halal practices and a love for Filipino food, Nurizam and Maharlika Food Street became a beloved part of the Taguig City community, leaving a tasty mark that would last for years.
